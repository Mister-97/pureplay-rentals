How Far in Advance Should You Book a Bounce House in Chicago?

6 min read  ·  Pure Play Rentals  ·  Chicago, IL  ·  March 11, 2026

Bounce house rentals in Chicago aren't always available on demand — especially during the busy summer season. If you're planning a birthday party, a block party, a school event, or any kind of outdoor celebration, knowing when to book can be the difference between getting exactly what you want and scrambling for a last-minute option. Here's a practical guide to booking windows in the Chicagoland area.

Peak Season: May Through September

Chicago's outdoor party season runs roughly from Memorial Day weekend through Labor Day, with the absolute busiest months being June, July, and August. During these months, rental companies are booked solid on weekends — especially Saturdays. Popular inflatable styles (waterslide combos, large obstacle courses) can be reserved months in advance for summer Saturdays.

If your party date falls in this window, the earlier you book the better. Waiting until three or four weeks before a July Saturday birthday isn't impossible, but your inflatable choices will be limited to whatever hasn't been claimed yet.

Booking Windows by Situation

Summer Saturday Party (June–August)

Book 4–8 weeks in advance to ensure you get your first choice of inflatable style and time slot. These dates go fastest.

Spring or Fall Weekend Party (April–May, September–October)

Book 2–4 weeks in advance. Demand is lower than summer, but weekends still fill up, especially around school holidays and spring break.

Weekday Event (Any Season)

Book 1–2 weeks in advance. Weekday availability is generally much better. Some companies offer lower weekday rates, though Pure Play's rate is the same year-round.

Last-Minute Booking (Under 1 Week)

Call and ask — we'll do our best. Cancellations do happen, and sometimes there's an opening. But don't count on a specific unit or time being available.

Why Weekends Fill Up So Fast

Chicago families tend to cluster birthday parties on Saturdays, which means rental companies are juggling multiple deliveries on the same day. A company with 10 inflatables might have all 10 out on a busy June Saturday. Add to that the setup and teardown time between rentals, and there's simply a hard ceiling on how many parties can be served on a given day.

The earlier you call, the wider your window of available time slots. Booking early also gives you more flexibility if you need to adjust your party start time after you've confirmed other details like catering or venue.
