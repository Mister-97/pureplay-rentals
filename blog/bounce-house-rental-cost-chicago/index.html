How Much Does a Bounce House Rental Cost in Chicago?

7 min read  ·  Pure Play Rentals  ·  Chicago, IL  ·  March 5, 2026

One of the first questions every parent asks when planning a bounce house party is: how much is this actually going to cost me? In the Chicago area, bounce house rental prices vary more than you might expect — and the cheapest quote doesn't always mean the best deal. Here's a transparent breakdown of what you'll typically pay, what drives the price up, and how Pure Play keeps things simple.

What's Usually Included (and What's Not)

A reputable bounce house company will include these in the base price:

  • Delivery to your location
  • Professional setup and staking
  • The blower motor and all necessary hardware
  • Full-day rental window (typically 6–8 hours)
  • Pickup and takedown at the end of the event

What might cost extra with some companies:

  • Delivery fee: Some companies charge $25–$75 if you're more than 15–20 miles from their base
  • Setup fee: A few budget operators add a separate labor charge
  • Generator rental: If your party location doesn't have a power outlet within 50 feet, you may need a generator — usually $75–$100 extra
  • Deposit: Most companies require a deposit of $50–$100 to hold your date, applied toward the total
  • Weekend surcharge: Saturday rentals sometimes cost $25–$50 more than weekday rates

Pure Play's approach: We charge a flat $300 for the entire rental — delivery, setup, blower, stakes, and takedown included. No weekend surcharges, no surprise delivery fees for Chicago and most suburbs. What you see is what you pay.

What Drives the Price Up?

Several factors influence how much a bounce house rental will cost you in Chicago:

  • Unit size and type: Larger combo units and waterslides cost more than a basic bounce house. More surface area, more materials, more setup time.
  • Rental duration: Many companies price by the hour. An 8-hour rental will cost significantly more than a 4-hour window with those companies. Pure Play's flat rate covers your full day.
  • Distance from the company's base: Companies in the south suburbs may charge extra to deliver to the northwest suburbs, and vice versa. Make sure you're booking from a company that actually serves your area without hidden distance fees.
  • Time of year: Peak season (May–September) sees higher demand. Some companies raise prices in summer — Pure Play keeps the rate consistent year-round.
  • Day of the week: Saturday bookings are the most competitive. Sunday and weekday rates are sometimes lower with other providers.

Is a $200 Bounce House Rental Worth It?

If you see a very low price on Craigslist or Facebook Marketplace, it's worth asking some questions. Budget operators sometimes cut corners on:

  • Cleaning and sanitizing between rentals
  • Insurance coverage (critical if there's an injury)
  • Unit inspection and maintenance
  • Delivery timing reliability

A bounce house that shows up late, smells like it hasn't been cleaned, or deflates mid-party ruins the event. The extra $50–$100 for a professional, insured, well-reviewed company is almost always worth it.

What About a Deposit?

Most companies, including Pure Play, require a deposit to secure your booking date. This is typically $50–$100 and is credited toward your total. Deposits are generally non-refundable if you cancel within a short window before the event — check the cancellation policy before you pay. Pure Play offers a full refund for cancellations made 7 or more days in advance.
