What's Included in a Bounce House Rental? (Chicago Edition)

6 min read  ·  Pure Play Rentals  ·  Chicago, IL  ·  March 13, 2026

Before you book a bounce house rental in Chicago, it's worth knowing exactly what you're getting for your money. Some companies bundle everything into one price. Others quote you a low rate and then add delivery fees, setup charges, and equipment costs on top. Here's a clear breakdown of what a quality bounce house rental should include — and what you might need to arrange separately.

What Should Always Be Included

A legitimate, professional bounce house rental company should include all of the following in their quoted price:

✓ Always Included with Pure Play

  • Delivery to your location
  • Professional setup by our team
  • Commercial-grade blower motor
  • Ground stakes and tie-downs
  • Full-day rental (up to 8 hours)
  • Pickup and teardown at end of event
  • Safety walkthrough at setup
  • Phone support during your event

✗ Not Included (What to Plan For)

  • Generator (if no outlet within 50 ft)
  • Extension cord over 50 ft
  • Tables, chairs, or party supplies
  • Water hookup for waterslides
  • Park permits (if applicable)
  • Food and beverages
  • Party supervision staff

Delivery, Setup, and Takedown — What Does That Actually Look Like?

Pure Play arrives approximately 60 minutes before your rental start time. Our setup crew handles everything: unloading the inflatable, positioning it in your designated area, staking it securely to the ground, and running the blower. We do a quick safety check, walk you through any key rules, and make sure everything is running properly before we leave.

At the end of your rental window, we return to take everything down. You don't need to deflate, fold, roll, or move anything. We pack it all up and clear the area — usually within 30–45 minutes. Your yard is back to normal by the time the last guest heads home.

The Blower: What You Need to Know

The blower motor is the heart of the inflatable — it runs continuously during the entire rental period to keep the bounce house inflated. It plugs into a standard 120V household outlet and draws about 15 amps. This means:

  • You need an outdoor electrical outlet (or an interior outlet accessible through a window or door)
  • The outlet must be within 50 feet of the inflatable setup location
  • The blower should be on a dedicated circuit — don't run it from the same outlet as a refrigerator or AC unit
  • The blower stays on the entire time the bounce house is in use — do not unplug it while kids are inside

If your outlet is more than 50 feet away, a heavy-duty extension cord rated for the amperage is required. We can advise you on what spec to get, or in some cases we can provide one — just let us know when you book.

What If You Don't Have a Nearby Outlet?

Park parties or venues without accessible outdoor power will need a generator. Generator rental is generally available from equipment rental shops in the Chicago area for around $75–$120 per day. If you're hosting at a park location, confirm with the park district whether generators are permitted on-site. Some parks have dedicated power pedestals available for reservation — it's worth asking when you apply for your park permit.

What to Prepare at Home Before Delivery

To make setup as fast and smooth as possible, do these things before our team arrives:

  • Clear the setup area: Remove lawn furniture, toys, sprinklers, garden hoses, and any debris from a space at least 20×20 feet (larger for combo units)
  • Mow the grass: Short grass makes staking easier and gives kids a better surface to walk on around the inflatable
  • Check for overhead hazards: Look for low-hanging branches, power lines, or awnings in the path of the inflatable — it needs clearance of 15+ feet overhead
  • Locate the nearest outlet: Know which outlet you plan to use and make sure it's accessible
  • Have an adult on-site: A responsible adult needs to be present when we arrive and when we pick up

Rental Duration: How Long Do You Get?

Pure Play's standard rental is up to 8 hours — plenty of time for a full afternoon birthday party. Most families run parties for 3–4 hours and then let the rental wind down before pickup. If you need a longer window, contact us and we'll see what we can arrange.

The rental clock starts from your requested start time, not from when we arrive to set up. We build in early arrival to make sure everything is running before your first guests arrive.
